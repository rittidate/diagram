Modify a noun with a noun

Noun Modifiers vs. Phrase or Clause Modifiers

MODIFYING NOUN — NOUN NOUN + MODIFYING OR CLAUSE

The same meaning can be expressed with a noun placed before the noun.

A noun can be modified by a phrase or clause placed after the noun.
PRE-POSITION MODIFIER POST POSITION MODIFIER
It is an office chair. (a chair for an office) It is a chair for an office. (Preppositional phase)
May I have a soup spoon. (a spoon for soup) May I have a spoon that is for soup. (Clause)
Ring the door bell. (the bell of the door) Ring the bell that is next to the door. (Clause)
It is a picnic table. (a table for a picnic) It is a table for picnics. (Preppositional phase)
He rides a mountain bike. (a bike for mountains) He rides a bike that was designed for the mountains. (Clause)
He is an Internal Revenue Service (IRS) agent. (an agent of the IRS) He is an agent of the Internal Revenue Service. a tax man (Preppositional phase)
He is a Kyoto man. (a Kyotan, Kyoto-ite?) (a man from Kyoto)

He is a man from Kyoto. (PP + proper noun)

Exceptional word form

PLURAL / WORD FORM ENDS IN "S" SINGULAR NOUN MODIFIER

In these words, the final "s" is part of the lexical base of the word.

More commonly, a singular noun modifies a noun.
It is a sports magazine / car. (Indicates variety) I bought a sport-utility vehicle. (other "sport" words)
We attended a jobs fair. (Indicates variety) I have a job interview today.
The measles outbreak made several children sick.
We went to a vegetables market. We went to a vegetable market.

Single vs Double-noun Modifiers

SINGLE NOUN MODIFIER DOUBLE-NOUN MODIFIER

A hyphen is not used when one noun is a modifier to another noun .

Two modifiers are linked with a hyphen to indicate relationship: (1) to link two words of equal importance, (2) to link two words modifying a noun that follows.
NO HYPHEN FOR EQUAL IMPORTANCE (noun–noun → noun)
He applied for a director position. He applied for a producer-director position.
The train station is closed. train (N) modifies ,station (N) The Menlo-Atherton train station is closed.
Lake Tahoe is on the Nevada border. Nevada (N) modifies ,border (N) Lake Tahoe is on the California-Nevada border.
FOR CLARITY (noun → noun–noun) FOR CLARITY (noun–noun → noun)
We bought two foot stools. two (det.) modifies foot (N) which modifies stools (N) We bought two-foot stools. See Hyphens.
