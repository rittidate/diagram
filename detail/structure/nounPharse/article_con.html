Article

Rule for using articles

  1. We use no article before some categories of nouns: games, meals, academic subjects and abstract nouns
  2. We use no article with plural nous for general statements;
    Lions come from Africa.
  3. We use a or an when we mention a singular countable noun for the first time or when it doesn't matter which one we mention.
  4. We use a or an when we describe someone's profession;
    She's a doctor.
  5. We use the for some places that are institutions; for example, the cinema, the theatre, the pub, the back and the post office, when the particular location is not important.
  6. We use the when something is unique in the world; for example, the Pacific and the Sydney Opera House.
  7. We use the before a singular or plural noun when we know what we are referring to;
    The house on the corner is for sale.
  8. We use the with some adjectives that describe general classes of people; for example, the poor, the unemployed and the wealthy.
  9. to say something about all the things referred to by a noun:

    The wolf is not really a dangerous animal (= Wolves are not really dangerous animals)
    The kangaroo is found only in Australia (= Kangaroos are found only in Australia)
    The heart pumps blood around the body. (= Hearts pump blood around bodies)

  10. We use the definite article in this way to talk about musical instruments:

    Joe plays the piano really well.(= Joe can play any piano)
    She is learning the guitar.(= She is learning to play any guitar)

  11. to refer to a system or service:

    How long does it take on the train.
    I heard it on the radio.
    You should tell the police.
